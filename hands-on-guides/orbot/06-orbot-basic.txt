# Start and stop Orbot

## Step 1

**Touch and hold** the grey **Orbot** icon in the centre of the screen until it turns yellow and says *Orbot is starting*.

![Activating Orbot](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/014.png)[Orbot img 15] ![Orbot is starting](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/015.png) [Orbot img 16]

*Figures 1 and 2: Activating Orbot*

## Step 2

The first time you start **Orbot** a notification will appear to confirm you connected successfully to the **Tor** network. **Tap** ![OK](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/016.png)[Orbot img 17] to see the green **Orbot** indication that **Orbot** is running.

![Orbot finishes connecting](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/017.png)[Orbot img 18] ![Connected to the Tor network](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/018.png)[Orbot img 19]

*Figures 3 and 4: Orbot finishes connecting*

**Note:** You will only see the notification screen (Fig. 3) the first time you start **Orbot** after installation.

## Step 3

To disconnect **Orbot** you **touch and hold** the green **Orbot** until it turns grey.  Or if you want to disconnect and quit **Orbot**, **tap** the menu icon (![menu icon](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/019.png)[Orbot img 20]) in the top right of the screen and select ![Exit](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/020.png)[Orbot img 21].

![Exiting Orbot](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/021.png)[Orbot img 22]

*Figures 5: Exiting Orbot*

In order to browse or chat on the internet anonymously, you need to install an app (browser or chat) which can route your communication through a proxy in conjunction with **Orbot**. Please read the related *Hands-on Guides* for using [**Orweb**](https://securityinabox.org/en/Orweb_main)[Orweb Main] and [**Gibberbot**](https://securityinabox.org/en/gibberbot_main)[Gibberbot Main] with **Orbot**.

## Step 4

If at any stage you want to appear to come from a new location, you can get a *new identity* from **Orbot** by **swiping** *left* or *right* on the green **Orbot** image.  The image will briefly spin and then display *You've switched to a new Tor Identity*.

![Getting a new Tor Identity](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/022.png)[Orbot img 23]

*Figure 6: Getting a new Tor Identity*

# Configure Orbot to use a Bridge

If **Tor** access is restricted or you wish to disguise the fact that you are using **Tor**, you can configure **Orbot** to use **bridges**.

## Step 1

**Tap** the ![](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/023.png)[Orbot img 24] icon at the top of the screen to be brought to the settings screen.

## Step 2

Scroll down to the **bridges** section and check the box next to *Use Bridges*.

![Enabling Bridges](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/024.png)[Orbot img 25]

*Figure 7: Enabling Bridges*

## Step 3

**Tap** the *Bridges* section underneath *Use Bridges* to be presented with a screen to enter the *IP address* of the *bridge* you want to use. Once correctly entered, **tap** ![OK](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/025.png)[Orbot img 26]. Restart **Orbot** to begin using the *bridge*.

![Adding a bridge](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/026.png)[Orbot img 27]

*Figure 8: Adding a bridge*

**Note:** to learn more about how to get bridge addresses see the [Tor Hands on Guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/tor_anonymitynetwork#3.3)[Tor Bridge Addresses].

# Configure Orbot to start automatically

To make sure you never forget to start **Orbot** it can be configured to start when you turn on your Android Device.

## Step 1

**Tap** the ![](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/023.png)[Orbot img 24] icon at the top of the screen to be brought to the settings screen.

## Step 2

**Check** the *Start Orbot on boot* option at the top of the settings screen.

![Starting Orbot automatically](https://securityinabox.org/sbox/screen/orbot-en-1/027.png)[Orbot img 28]

*Figure 9: Starting Orbot automatically*
